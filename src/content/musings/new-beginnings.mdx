Every so often, I get the urge to redesign my personal website.

My first experience with web development was in middle school, playing around with HTML and CSS in my free time and learning syntax from a book.
I've come a long way since then (and so has programming as a whole), but my love for expressing myself online is as strong as ever.

I'm a huge [Notion](https://www.notion.com/) enthusiast, and it felt like a fitting theme for my latest revamp.
I've been ruminating on this design concept for [a while](https://x.com/brennenho_/status/2017000862326149516), and I finally got around to bringing it to life (thanks Codex).

<Callout icon="🔎" color="green">
  All previous iterations of my personal site are on my GitHub
  [here](https://github.com/brennenho/brennen.dev/branches/all) and
  [here](https://github.com/brennenho/brennen.dev-legacy).
</Callout>

I had three priorities when reimagining this site:

- create something that felt unique and reflective of myself
- make it interactive
- make a public-facing blog

I was especially excited about the third point.

For years, I've wanted a place to jot down thoughts for anyone and everyone.
In fact, some of the first websites I ever created were WordPress blog sites.
As AI becomes more and more integrated into my workflow, I wanted a reason to practice writing for myself more.
I also wanted a place to brain dump online. This will hopefully serve both purposes.

However, this won't be a traditional blog.
I may occasionally post life updates, but I'm not committing to anything.
I also hope to write more about things I'm thinking about, hence the "musings" label.
Who knows if anyone will end up reading these.
Maybe I'm writing to the ether, and the usefulness of these posts will end at training GPT-7.

Hopefully more to come!
